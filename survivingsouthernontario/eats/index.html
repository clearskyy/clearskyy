eats
Tim Horton's
aka Timmy's, Tim's etc, is the home to many canadians; mostly because their coffee is the equalivent of a harsh narcotic. I will admit that they do not put narcotics in their beverages but your immediate addiction may prove otherwise. The amazing taste the the caffine buzz associated with each steaming cup of coffee will drive you to have withdrawls and crave it whenever you don't have a cup in your hand which will probably be never considering there is one almost every block.
Poutine
Nothing will satisfy the cravings and supply you with the proper amount of internal insoluation quite as well as the poutine. As we all know the poutine is the national food of Canada and the areas so lucky to surround its boarders. Not only is it tasty, it is served in about every single resteraunt you will encounter and if not; it will become a part of the menu in due time. One would wonder how it is you can add varation to such perfection and the answer is you really can't. However this hasn't stop a select few shops from giving it a shot: One such venture is to add green peppers, onions and beef to the mix and call it the "Kodiac" poutine; which is amazing by the way and is served at this particular resteraunt which is not in Canada so I'm not sure why I'm mentioning it besides the fact that it is delicous. At any rate; you could argue that it is just fries, gravy and cheese curds and you would be correct if you didn't notice the sparkle of Canadian pride (and soul, oh tasty tasty souls) put into each of these meal subsitutes; for that it makes it all the better.
Billy K's
Now I'm not entirely sure if this is (and most likely will be) a local thing but you have to find one of these places if they do in face exist else where; if not just come here. These 50 cent "steamies" (mass produced hot dogs from hell) will have your taste buds begging for more. (well you'll end up buying 50 of them anyways so no real problem here I suppose, they are 50cents after all.) Not only is most of the food dirt cheap; its actually good, its like a massive chip stand turned resteraunt. that brings us to our next topic:
Chip Stands
These buggers are everywhere; they are most likely over priced but they are amazingly good and amazingly terrible for you, just about everything on the menu is good (with the proper condiment mind you) you will not walk away disappointed you stopped at one of these (always sketchy looking) food stands.
Sports Bars
There are plenty of these guys and they are all amazing. You feel immediately undressed for entering one of these bad-boys; but that won't stop the warm welcome and the promise of always great food. I personally love the multiple flat screens showing various sports being played and chowing down on some great (and I will admit a tad pricey) food. They also have beer there which is always a plus; and not only do they have beer, but cute girls serving said beer, which brings me to my next point..
Beer
"its liquid bread; its good for you." (assuming your of age, kids close your eyes) We make it and we make it good (the best I might add) Now I'm not trying to pull some patriotic nonsense here and say a bunch of things im just stating facts (not facts; complete opinion here, like most of this site.) the beer made in Canada is the most delicious thing in the entire world (assuming you actually like beer that is) I'm more than certain that Labatt is the greatest thing since Molson (both are great) and whatever else you find, your bound to have a good time (and a cheap one at that) So drink up and enjoy the splender.
A&W
This is Canada at its finest. (with the exception of Tim's of course) This is how you properly take an American fast food franchise and make it infinately better. Not only is the portions larger, and the cashiers cuter (depending on what time/day exactly you decide to show up) but the taste is exponentionally better (with the addition of certain seasoning I have yet to see in the American version of A&W). Not only that but the menu includes items I have yet to see added to the American menu; one of which, the Whistle Dog is a atery clogging gift from the heavens. Now this isn't a bash on a franchiese but rather a slight brag and a hint to the US to step up their game.
McDonalds
"McDonalds is only good for drunk food" and while this is a really true statement given by a ex-coworker of mine I have to say that McDonalds is good just about anywhere but and this is more or less a thing of preference but I find that the McD's on the Canadian side to be a tad better; now they are basically identical except that they don't put the seasoning on their burgers that I find odd everytime I bite into a American McD's burger. Now its not terrible (not in the slightest) however I would perfer my burger to contain just a little less pepper please and thank you.
