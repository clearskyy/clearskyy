what to advoid
The Snow
Now I realize this is more of something you pray to not run into considering advoiding snow in Ontario is completely impossible; it will find you, where ever you hide it will find you. The fact remains that its Canada and unless global warming becomes more than some myth there will be snow; lots of it. Now its pretty and white and sparkly and when it acutally snows out it means that the weather is actually around tolerable, but this is beyond the point I am trying to make.
Not Spelling Things with U's
We will notice; don't ask me why we do this (I'm not entirely sure myself) but it will be pointed out if you write colour without that very essential letter.
The "bud" trend
saying bud at the end of your sentences is a great way to be friendly with strangers but for your own sanity; I suggest to just try and advoid saying it, for you will find it very difficult to stop and we would perfer it if you just said, "eh" instead (its a dead give away of where you went to vacation)
Wandering around the residential areas
It's just really boring out there and a complete waste of time unless you know people out there. Who wants to stare at houses when you could be out in downtown having loads of fun anyways? Not to say there aren't some nice houses out there, but in all honesty its like anywhere else; nothing special really unless your visiting someone out there in which case that is differnt. Plus you don't want to break up all the street hockey games in the streets.
Insulting Hockey
not only is this stupid; it will probably get you killed or kicked out of where you decided to do this act; why would you want to get banned from the most amazing place in the planet? Its not only a sport. Its our livelyhood, our childhood, what we look forward to, and what we drink to. its our bread and butter and wearing enemy colours will probably get you some strange looks/taunts. Just advoid at all costs please (we can't be reasoned with as hard as you try, its just woven in our DNA)
Quebec
yes, I understand this about surviving Ontario; but the fact remains that you do not want to go there unless you are absolutely sure you are fluent in french and have a GPS capible of decyrpting their horrific highway system; there are highways that lead there and the difference is made almost complete evident as soon as you pass the sign into this adjacent province as the quality/style of everything changes dramatically immediately. It's not a terrible place, and it can be enjoyed, just advoid for Canadian novices.
